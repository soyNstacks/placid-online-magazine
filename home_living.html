Slow and Minimal Living in a Fast-paced World

What is Slow Living? Slow Living is defined as

"a lifestyle that emphasises a slower approach to aspects of everyday life, and movements or actions at a relaxed pace".

It also incorporates the mindset of Simple and Minimal Living, both of which are characterised by individuals to being satisfied with what they have rather than want. A one-eighty change has been made when the Covid-19 pandemic happened in the blink of an eye. Appointments and plans were cancelled, even the most unpredictable working habits were forced to transform into a stay-home event. That might probably have also been the cause for an eye-opener to the clutters of mess in the area we call Home. There has been a visible rise in interest for slow lifestyle in the past year, so much that tips and tricks are seen everywhere on social platforms like Youtube, Instagram and Pinterest. This lifestyle has been hailed upon by many as an 'aesthetic by many that advocates for personal well-being in conjunction with minimal goods consumption.

The first key element to slow living is going minimal. This can be activated in homes, with simple and less decor or storage. Recycle or donate items that are deemed unnecessary in your crib so that you can achieve minimalism, and at the same time, reduce wastage.

Calm and free the mind from the usual frenzy to adopt a slower approach in everyday life. Withdrawing from social media has been proven by like-minded people to cut down unwanted toxicity from strangers and acquaintances. Consuming too much glamour and idealism can be taxing on one's mental health.

Recently, there has been a challenge well-recieved by many. It encourages minimal living by setting different goals to achieve in seven days. Our in-house editor at Placid had initially decided to join for the fun but ended up finding it surprisingly beneficial.

A slower approach can be in the form of savouring moments of life. Fresh coffee beans glistering from natural oils poured into a grinder, and out comes finely-sized brown coffee powder. Empty the powder into a cotton filter hitched on ceramic and heat. Water flows from the goose-necked kettle into the ceramic and pot in a consistent circular motion. As liquid drips into the fine china mug, the homely abode is filled with aromatic flavour of Brazilian coffee. The blank cotton canvas is left with a heap of drenched residue. A sip of the liquid in the china leaves a clear yet floral aftertaste. Picking up a Marie Kondo black and white book while relishing the distinctive fragrance of coffee... There a brand new day starts with a fresh mind and palate.

Slow lifestyle has made phenomenal changes, at gradual pace, to some people's lives in terms of habits, mental health as well as overall well-being. A stroll in the morning along the countryside rather than waking up from bed after half a day has passed and having missed appointments or tasks. Taking in the fresh air and doing self-reflection may improve one's mental health with positivity. Purchasing necessary items instead of splurging on temporary wants could also reduce wastage and uneeded expenses. Overall, there is a sense of 'cleansing' when a simpler lifestyle is adopted.

To find out more about slow and minimal living, visit Sloww. To learn more about the community, head to Simple Living and Minimalism on Reddit.

Who are we?

Placid is an independent magazine created to advocate for the theme of Slow Living, with a mission to add a touch of calm to individuals who are struck by the effects of world issues amidst the already hectic life. At the same time, the magazine also hopes to contribute to efforts against the deteriorating health of Gaia.

Slow Living, like its name, is a lifestyle that emphasises a slower approach to aspects of everyday life, and movements or actions at a relaxed pace. We hope this online magazine would help broaden perspectives and lend a helping hand amidst the global pandemic.
